# 📊 HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard  
**Why Employees Leave: A Data‑Driven Attrition Analysis**

This project analyzes the key drivers behind employee attrition using a real HR dataset.  
The dashboard summarizes **1470 employees**, **237 leavers**, and an overall **attrition rate of 16.12%**.

## 🔍 Key Insights

- **Tenure (2–5 years)** shows the highest attrition peak, followed by employees leaving within the **first year**.  
- The **26–35 age group** is the most affected, indicating a young but ambitious workforce seeking growth.  
- Employees earning **0–3000** represent the largest share of leavers, highlighting compensation issues at entry‑level.  
- **R&D and Sales** concentrate most exits, suggesting high workload or management challenges.

## 🚀 Actionable Insights

- Strengthen onboarding during the first 4 weeks and introduce 30/60/90‑day HR check‑ins.  
- Review entry‑level compensation and introduce automatic salary adjustments after 12 months.  
- Define clear career paths and internal mobility opportunities for employees aged 26–35.  
- Provide managerial training and workload assessment in high‑pressure departments (R&D, Sales).  
- Implement targeted retention programs for employees in their **first 12 months** and **2–5 year** tenure window.

## 📁 Dataset
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition (Kaggle)
